About Barton

The Barton Reading and Spelling System® is a multi-sensory, research and evidence based program proven to help individuals who struggle with reading and/or spelling. This system allows tutors, like myself, the ability to provide one-on-one intensive and explicit instruction. By utilizing this specialized instruction, I am able to make reading and spelling a possibility for those bright and hardworking individuals who just learn differently. The methods we teach are multi-sensory and more effective than what is currently being offered to students who are struggling in this area.

Following is a partial list of some of the challenges your student may be displaying:

  • Inaccurate spelling
  • Slow or inaccurate reading
  • Writes some letters or numbers backwards
  • Can't memorize multiplication tables
  • Trouble telling time on a clock with hands
  • Unusual pencil grip and letter formation
  • Difficulty learning to tie shoes

Dyslexia Signs

In Preschool

  • Delayed speech
  • Mixing up the sounds and syllables in long words
  • Chronic ear infections
  • Severe reactions to childhood illnesses
  • Constant confusion of left vs. right
  • Late establishing a dominant hand
  • Difficulty learning to tie shoes
  • Trouble memorizing their address, phone number, or the alphabet
  • Can't create words that rhyme
  • Has a close relative with dyslexia

In Elementary School

  • Dysgraphia (slow, non-automatic handwriting that is difficult to read)
  • Letter or number reversals continuing past the end of first grade
  • Extreme difficulty learning cursive
  • Slow, choppy, inaccurate reading:
    • skips or misreads prepositions (at, to, of)
    • guesses based on shape or context
  • Terrible spelling
  • Often can't remember sight words (they, were, does) or homonyms (their, they're, and there)
  • Trouble with math
    • memorizing multiplication tables
  • Dreads going to school

In High School

  • Limited vocabulary
  • Extremely poor written expression
    • large discrepancy between verbal skills and written compositions
  • Unable to master a foreign language
  • Difficulty reading printed music
  • Poor grades in many classes
  • May drop out of high school

In Adulthood

  • Slow reader
  • May have to read a page 2 or 3 times to understand it
  • Terrible speller
  • Difficulty putting thoughts onto paper
    • Dreads writing memos or letters
  • Still has difficulty with left vs. right
  • Often gets lost, even in a familiar city
  • Sometimes confuses 'b' and 'd', especially when tired or sick

* These warning signs reprinted with prior written permission from Susan Barton, Founder of Bright Solutions for Dyslexia and developer of the Barton Reading and Spelling System®

Barton Screening

A Barton student must:

  • be at least 5 years old and in kindergarten.
  • speak and comprehend spoken English.
  • have an IQ of 70 or higher.
  • struggle with spelling when writing stories, and cannot easily sound out unknown words when reading.
  • pass our 10-minute student screening.

About Cindy…

"These students are extremely bright. They just needed to be taught differently through explicit, multi-sensory methods…they need to be shown they can do it."

My name is Cindy Deagen and I am a Certified Barton Tutor. I have had the privilege of being a Tutor Specialist using the Barton Reading & Spelling System® for five years. My journey as a Barton tutor began as a volunteer with our local schools. I quickly became aware of each struggling student's potential for success while becoming better readers and spellers through the Barton program. These students are extremely bright. They just needed to be taught differently through explicit, multi-sensory methods.

I currently own and operate my own tutoring business, called Reading and Spelling Success, and continue to experience the joy and satisfaction in helping my students succeed in reading and spelling. I am constantly amazed at the number of bright and hardworking students who think they are not smart simply because they find reading and spelling difficult. However, as a result of this wonderful intervention program, my students gain self-confidence and “I can do it” frames of mind. These students need more than being told they can do it, they need to be shown they can do it.

My early years in college studying child development taught me the importance of reading to children. Fortunately, this knowledge helped to keep a continuous flow of books in our home and many fun-filled trips to our local libraries.

In addition to working with my students, my passions in life include our family, friends, our Therapy Dog, Harley, and his little buddy, Bear. Harley and I especially like working with Paws to Read at the Pleasanton Public Library where kids sign up and spend time relaxing and reading to the dogs. I’m also a volunteer Booklegger with the Pleasanton Public Library. As Bookleggers, we bring our totes full of books to share with the students at the elementary and middle schools within our school district. In addition to being an advocate for reading, I also enjoy painting, color pencil art, education (we never stop learning), travel, hiking, kayaking and anything encompassing nature. My favorite mottos are “Live, Laugh, Love” and this quote of Mahatma Gandhi “Be the change you wish to see in the world.”
